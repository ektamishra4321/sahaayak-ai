## Types of Returns: RTO vs Customer Returns (RVP)
Two kinds of return shipments exist. RTO (Return to Origin) is when the courier
could not deliver the parcel and it comes back to you before reaching the
customer. RVP / customer return is when the buyer returns the product after
delivery. Customers can return a product within 7 days from the date of
delivery. Both types together are treated as "Return Shipments" under the
claims policy, but compensation and fees differ: customer returns carry a
return shipping fee based on shipment weight, while RTO carries no extra
shipping fee.

## Receiving a Return: Protect Yourself First
When a return parcel arrives, inspect the outer packaging BEFORE opening.
If it looks tampered - torn or damaged outer packaging, extra taping beyond
the courier's own branded tape, or a label that appears cut and pasted from
another shipment - you must record the remark "Tampered/Damaged" on the
digital proof of delivery (e-POD). If no remark is made on the e-POD, Meesho
treats the parcel as having arrived intact, which weakens any later claim.
Record a single continuous unboxing video: show the label clearly, all sides
of the parcel, then open it without pausing. Meesho does not accept edited
videos or videos with cuts. Also take 3-4 clear photos of product, packaging,
and the shipping label (AWB).

## Raising a Return Claim (Wrong / Used / Damaged / Missing Item)
If the returned item is wrong, used, damaged, or missing:
1. Do not restock the item.
2. In Supplier Panel go to the Support section, category "Return/RTO &
   Exchange", select the matching issue (e.g. wrong or damaged return), and
   raise a ticket/claim for that order.
3. Attach the uncut unboxing video, photos, and dispatch proof.
4. Raise the claim quickly - seller guides consistently cite a window of
   roughly 48-72 hours from receiving the return [VERIFY the exact current
   window shown on the return record in your panel]. Late claims are rejected.

## Compensation for Approved Claims
Since mid-2022 Meesho reimburses a "fair value" of the product based on
category industry benchmarks - approximately your procurement cost, not your
full selling price with margin. For damaged (rather than wrong) returns, the
payout is a percentage of that fair value, scaling roughly from 10% for
minimal damage up to 90% for significant damage. Sellers often ask when the claim money (paisa) will arrive: approved
amounts are settled to your bank account within about 7 working days of
approval and appear under
the Compensation and Recoveries tab in the Supplier Panel.

## If Your Claim Is Rejected
You can reopen the ticket or email seller support with stronger evidence
(clearer photos, the uncut video, invoice). Note: an unusually high
claim-to-return ratio compared to your category can trigger an account
investigation, so only raise genuine, well-evidenced claims.
